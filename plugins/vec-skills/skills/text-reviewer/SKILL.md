# text-reviewer

Reviews written text and improves clarity, grammar, concision, and tone, while preserving the author's intent and voice.

## When to use
- The user asks to review, proofread, or improve a piece of text.
- They want an email, message, or document to read more clearly or more professionally.
- They ask for a second opinion on their writing.

## How to review
1. **Read for intent first.** Identify the goal and the audience before changing any words.
2. **Fix the essentials:** grammar, spelling, agreement, punctuation.
3. **Improve clarity:** turn long sentences into direct ones; cut redundancy and needless jargon.
4. **Match the tone** to the context (formal, neutral, or casual) without erasing the author's voice.
5. **Preserve meaning.** Never invent information or change what the author meant.

## How to deliver
- Return the **revised version** first, ready to copy.
- Then list the **main changes** as short bullets (what and why).
- If something is ambiguous and only the author can resolve it, ask instead of guessing.
